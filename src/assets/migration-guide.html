Migration Guide — Tally & Zoho Books to SelfAccounting.AI

How to migrate your opening balances when switching from Tally or Zoho Books

Overview

If you are migrating from Tally or Zoho Books, you can bring your opening balances into SelfAccounting.AI using our Bulk Import feature. This guide walks you through the process.

Approach: Export your closing trial balance from your current system, then import it as opening balance journal entries. This ensures your books continue seamlessly from your cutover date.

← Back to Onboarding Guide

Step 1: Choose Your Cutover Date

Pick a date from which you will start using SelfAccounting.AI—typically the first day of a month or fiscal year (e.g., 1 Jan 2025). All transactions before this date remain in your old system for reference.

Step 2: Export Trial Balance from Tally or Zoho

From Tally

  1. Open Tally and go to Display → Trial Balance
  2. Set the period to end on the day before your cutover date
  3. Export to Excel or CSV (usually via Export or right-click)

From Zoho Books

  1. Log in to Zoho Books
  2. Go to Reports → Trial Balance
  3. Select the date range ending the day before your cutover date
  4. Click Export and choose CSV or Excel

You need columns for: Account Name, Debit, and Credit (or Debit Balance / Credit Balance).

Step 3: Complete SelfAccounting.AI Setup

Before importing, complete the basics:

  • Register and onboard your organization (if not already done)
  • Ensure your Bulk Import feature is enabled (contact your account manager if you don't see the option)
  • Add any custom ledger accounts under Settings → Ledger Accounts if your chart of accounts differs from the defaults

Step 4: Prepare Your CSV for Import

Convert your trial balance to our CSV format. Each line represents one journal entry:

debitAccount,creditAccount,amount,entryDate,description

Map your Tally/Zoho account names to our codes:

Tip: For opening balances, use owner_shareholder_account as the balancing account (the "Opening Balance Equity" equivalent). Do not use Retained Earnings—it is system-calculated.

Example CSV

debitAccount,creditAccount,amount,entryDate,description
bank,owner_shareholder_account,100000,2025-01-01,Opening balance - Bank
cash,owner_shareholder_account,5000,2025-01-01,Opening balance - Cash
accounts_receivable,owner_shareholder_account,25000,2025-01-01,Opening balance - Sundry Debtors

Step 5: Import in SelfAccounting.AI

  1. Log in to SelfAccounting.AI
  2. Go to Expenses → Journal Entries
  3. Click Bulk Import (if the button is visible—your license must have this feature enabled)
  4. Paste your CSV data or use the template
  5. Review the preview—fix any errors shown
  6. Click Import to create the journal entries

Step 6: Verify & Go Live

  • Run a Trial Balance report in SelfAccounting.AI (Reports → Trial Balance)
  • Compare it with your exported trial balance—totals should match
  • From your cutover date onward, record all new transactions in SelfAccounting.AI

Support: For migration assistance, contact your account manager or support@selfaccounting.ai.
